# Founder Beta Testing & Analytics Spec — Leasing Operations Panel

## Purpose

This document defines exactly what the Leasing Operations Panel should log, measure, and surface during the internal founder beta so the product generates **useful evidence, operational insight, and AI-analyzable context** before being offered to other landlords.

This is not just a QA plan.
This is a **product validation and evidence-generation system**.

The goal is for the app to help answer:

1. Does this save meaningful time?
2. Does this create cleaner leasing data?
3. Does this improve leasing follow-up and outcomes?
4. What product recommendations should another AI be able to generate from the data?

---

# 1. Core Beta Philosophy

## Product rule

The app should not only help process leads.
It should also generate **rich operational telemetry** so the founder can later ask another AI:

* Where am I losing leads?
* What steps take too long?
* What fields are often missing?
* What should the product automate next?
* What would be most valuable to sell to landlords?

## Design requirement

Every important action in the system should generate a **context-rich event log**.

Not just:

* “Lead updated”

But:

* what changed
* who changed it
* when
* what the AI suggested before the human changed it
* what communication triggered the change
* what happened next

This is what makes later AI analysis actually useful.

---

# 2. Beta Objectives

## During internal testing, the system should help prove:

### Operational value

* lead intake is faster
* lead review is more consistent
* fewer leads are dropped or forgotten

### Data value

* leasing conversations become structured records
* records are usable for export / CRM entry
* AI is actually helping rather than creating cleanup work

### Commercial value

* there is a measurable problem being solved
* there are clear buyer pain points
* the product creates outcomes another landlord would pay for

# 6. Context Requirements for Logs (Very Important)

## Product rule

Logs must preserve enough context for later AI analysis.

For important events, include:

* account_id
* property_id
* prospect_id
* interaction_id
* destination Twilio number
* source type (sms / call / voicemail)
* lead status before and after
* AI suggestion before human edit
* human-confirmed value after edit
* timing information
* whether the lead was new or existing
* whether the record was export-ready at the time

## Example

If a user changes `desired_move_in_date`, do NOT only log:

* “field edited”

Log:

```json
{
  "field_name": "desired_move_in_date",
  "old_value": "April",
  "new_value": "2026-04-15",
  "edit_reason": "clarified during review",
  "ai_original_value": "April",
  "confidence": 0.71
}
```

This is what makes downstream AI recommendations valuable.

# 7. KPI Framework — What the App Must Measure

This section defines the KPIs the founder should be able to see automatically.

---

# 8. Core KPI Category A — Lead Capture Completeness

## Goal

Measure whether the system is actually catching all inbound leasing communications.

## KPIs

* total inbound SMS received
* total inbound calls received
* total voicemail transcripts attached
* total communication events ingested
* total webhook failures
* duplicate webhook rate
* percent of inbound events successfully attached to a prospect
* percent of inbound events successfully attributed to a property

## Founder question answered

“Am I actually capturing the leasing funnel reliably?”

## Recommended target

* 98%+ of inbound Twilio events successfully captured

---

# 9. Core KPI Category B — Speed / Time Savings

## Goal

Measure whether the product is actually saving operational time.

## KPIs

* average time from inbound communication → first review
* average time from inbound communication → structured record complete
* average time from inbound communication → export ready
* median review completion time for SMS
* median review completion time for calls / voicemail
* number of leads reviewed per day
* average number of edits per reviewed lead

## Founder question answered

“Is this materially faster than my current workflow?”

## Recommended target

* simple SMS lead processed in under 2 minutes
* call / voicemail lead processed in under 5 minutes

---

# 10. Core KPI Category C — AI Usefulness

## Goal

Measure whether the AI is helping or creating cleanup work.

## KPIs

* percent of AI summaries accepted without edit
* percent of AI summaries edited
* average number of field edits after AI extraction
* extraction failure rate
* top 10 most frequently corrected fields
* average AI confidence by field type
* percentage of interactions with at least 3 useful extracted fields
* percentage of interactions where AI provided no useful structured data

## Founder question answered

“Is the AI reducing work enough to justify its existence?”

## Recommended interpretation

The most corrected fields often indicate:

* prompt issues
* schema issues
* UI issues
* product opportunities

---

# 11. Core KPI Category D — Workflow Adherence

## Goal

Measure whether the founder/team actually use the app consistently.

## KPIs

* percent of inbound leads opened in review screen
* percent of inbound leads completed to reviewed status
* percent of leads marked export-ready
* percent of leads that remain in New or Needs Review for over 24 hours
* average backlog count by day
* number of leads snoozed
* number of leads reopened

## Founder question answered

“Is this becoming the real operating workflow or am I reverting to old habits?”

---

# 12. Core KPI Category E — Data Quality / Structure Quality

## Goal

Measure whether the resulting data is actually clean and usable.

## KPIs

* percent of leads with property assigned
* percent of leads with status assigned
* percent of leads with at least one contact method
* percent of leads with move-in timing captured
* percent of leads with budget captured
* percent of leads with unit type captured
* percent of leads with enough fields to export cleanly
* duplicate prospect rate

## Founder question answered

“Is this producing trustworthy CRM-ready data?”

---

# 13. Core KPI Category F — Funnel / Leasing Outcome Metrics

## Goal

Measure whether the product improves leasing process outcomes.

## KPIs

* total new leads
* total qualified leads
* total tour requests
* total tours scheduled
* total likely applications
* total applications
* total approvals
* total leases signed
* total closed/lost leads
* conversion rates between each stage

## Founder question answered

“Is this improving leasing pipeline visibility and outcomes?”

## Important note

Even if some downstream outcomes are entered manually at first, they are still very valuable to capture.

---

# 14. Core KPI Category G — Founder Commercial Insight Metrics

## Goal

Capture evidence that can later be used in product positioning and sales.

## KPIs / evidence points

* leads that would likely have been lost without the app
* number of times same-day review changed outcome
* number of times AI surfaced important info the user may have missed
* number of prospects successfully organized from messy communications
* properties with highest communication chaos
* average admin burden per lead

## Founder question answered

“What exact painkiller am I actually selling?”

# 17. AI-Ready Analytics Export Requirements

## Product requirement

The founder should be able to export the beta data in a format another AI can analyze for recommendations.

## Required export types

### 1. KPI summary export

A structured summary of key metrics by day/week/month

### 2. Event log export

Raw but structured event history

### 3. Prospect lifecycle export

One row/object per prospect showing the full lead journey

### 4. AI correction export

One row/object per AI extraction showing what AI guessed vs what human confirmed

Useful for giving to another AI for high-level recommendations.

## Include

* date range
* total inbound communications
* review speed metrics
* AI usefulness metrics
* funnel metrics
* export metrics
* top missing fields
* top corrected fields
* property-level performance summaries

## Example structure

```json
{
  "date_range": {
    "start": "",
    "end": ""
  },
  "summary": {
    "total_inbound_sms": 0,
    "total_inbound_calls": 0,
    "avg_minutes_to_first_review": 0,
    "avg_minutes_to_export_ready": 0,
    "ai_summary_acceptance_rate": 0,
    "avg_field_edits_per_lead": 0,
    "review_completion_rate": 0,
    "export_ready_rate": 0
  },
  "top_corrected_fields": [],
  "top_missing_fields": [],
  "funnel": {},
  "by_property": []
}
```

# 20. Export Format C — Prospect Lifecycle JSON

## Purpose

This is probably the most valuable format for AI analysis.

## Each prospect record should include

* prospect metadata
* source number / property context
* all interactions
* AI outputs
* human edits
* timeline of status changes
* notes
* export state
* downstream outcome fields

## Why

This lets another AI answer:

* what lead patterns convert best
* where friction occurs
* what fields matter most
* which workflows should be automated next

# 21. Export Format D — AI Correction Analysis JSON

## Purpose

Measure AI usefulness precisely.

## Each record should include

* interaction_id
* source_type
* extracted field name
* AI value
* AI confidence
* human-confirmed value
* whether it changed

## Why

This is one of the most valuable product-improvement datasets you can generate.

# 22. Weekly Founder Reflection System

## Product requirement

Add a simple founder-only form or note area to capture qualitative learnings during beta.

## Create a table called `founder_observations`

Fields:

* id
* account_id
* user_id
* observation_date
* observation_type
* title
* body
* related_property_id nullable
* related_prospect_id nullable
* tags_json nullable
* created_at

## Suggested observation types

* workflow_friction
* product_idea
* missed_lead_risk
* ai_issue
* ui_issue
* customer_value_signal
* future_sales_claim

## Suggested weekly reflection prompts

Once per week, prompt the founder to answer:

1. What part of the workflow felt most annoying this week?
2. What did the app save me from doing manually?
3. What information did I still wish I had?
4. What did the AI get wrong most often?
5. What would another landlord be most excited to pay for?
6. What nearly caused me to lose a lead this week?
7. What should be automated next?

These answers are very useful for later AI analysis and product direction.

# 23. Testing Instructions for Founder Beta

## Beta should be run in real operational conditions.

Do not just simulate fake usage.
Use actual inbound leasing traffic where possible.

## Testing behavior rules

The founder / internal testers should try to:

* process every inbound Twilio leasing lead through the app
* avoid handling leads outside the app when possible
* mark statuses honestly
* add notes when friction occurs
* use export-ready workflow consistently
* log weekly reflections

## Important

The point is not perfect discipline.
The point is to capture where real-world workflow breaks down.
